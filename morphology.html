Morphology

Twenty roots, thousands of words.

Reading Helix · Volume 8

The Blueprint

Morphology is the study of meaning-bearing word parts: roots, prefixes, and suffixes. The Latin root spec (to see) shows up in inspect, prospect, retrospect, spectacle, perspective, conspicuous, suspect — and dozens more. Twenty well-chosen Greek and Latin roots unlock thousands of words in the academic vocabulary. For the older reader pushing into trades, science, law, or medicine, morphology is vocabulary multiplication.

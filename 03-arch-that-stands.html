Before You Play — The Secret of the Arch

A flat stone beam fails under compression — it cracks. An arch converts that downward force into outward thrust, spreading the load into the walls on either side. Every stone is wedge-shaped (called a voussoir). The keystone at the top locks them all in place.

The arch doesn't pull. It pushes. Remove the keystone and the whole structure fails. That keystone is also called the boss. Now you know where the word comes from.

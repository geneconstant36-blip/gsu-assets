Why We Play

Civic literacy is not a trivia game. It is the working knowledge that lets an adult American recognize a right when it applies, name a jurisdiction when it matters, and see a founding principle in the situations of ordinary life — a traffic stop, a workplace conversation about wages, a public protest, a state law that quarrels with a federal one.

Most Americans went through school without ever being asked to reason from the Constitution to a real situation. The Compass is the missing exercise. It teaches by testing, and it grades honestly — no participation trophies, no dressed-up quiz masquerading as understanding. Miss a question and you get the reasoning back in plain English, plus a specific place in the free GSU library to shore it up. Clear the bar and you have proven something real about yourself: you can carry the Constitution into your daily life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are donations tax-deductible?
Yes. The Foundation for Global Instruction is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit (EIN 39-2716552). Your donation is tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law. You will receive a PayPal receipt you can use for your records.
What is the difference between donating and buying books on Amazon?
When you donate, your money goes directly to the Foundation and you receive books at no additional cost. When you buy on Amazon, KDP pays royalties to the Foundation as the author account holder. Both support the mission. The donation path gives you more titles per dollar and a tax deduction. The Amazon path supports the platform infrastructure that makes free PDFs possible.
Can I donate books to a school, library, or shelter instead of keeping them?
Yes — and we encourage it. Several donors have used the $100 tier to stock a shelter reading shelf or a prison library. If you are donating to an institution, let us know and we can include a letter from the Foundation on official letterhead.
What is the Wall of Gratitude and how do I get on it?
The Wall of Gratitude honors people who move GSU books into the hands of learners through the Amazon affiliate program. There is no accounting department and no commissions — when someone buys through your shared link, Amazon reports it and your count rises. Email the Foundation to receive your affiliate link. It costs nothing.
Why "Quid Pro Quo"?
Because charity that creates dependency dishonors both the giver and the receiver. Quid pro quo — something for something — respects everyone in the transaction. You give something real. You receive something real. The Foundation advances its mission. Nobody owes anyone anything after the exchange. That is sovereignty.

The Sovereign Transcript

Your child's learning, recorded as a transcript and portfolio you can print, sign, and keep — built entirely on your own device. The record belongs to your family, not to any institution.

How this works — and why it is different. This page reads your child's game and lesson progress straight from this browser, on this device, where Global Sovereign University already saved it with no account required. By default this tool makes no network calls at all — nothing is uploaded, nothing is tracked, nothing leaves your computer. You add the student's name and your own portfolio entries, then print or save a clean report. Because the data lives on this device, the transcript reflects the work done here; if your child also studied on a tablet or library computer, run this tool there too and combine the printouts.

1 · Whose record is this?

These details are saved on this device only, so the page remembers them next time. They never leave your browser.

2 · What GSU recorded on this device

Badges are earned by lifetime correct answers in each game: Bronze at 10, Silver at 25, Gold at 50, Platinum at 100.

3 · Your portfolio — everything else they learned

A real portfolio is more than game scores. Add the books they read, the field trips you took, the projects they built, the volunteer hours, the experiments that worked (and the ones that didn't). These become part of the printed record.

No portfolio entries yet. Add your first above.

4 · Print or save your record

"Print / Save as PDF" opens your browser's print dialog — choose your printer, or choose "Save as PDF" as the destination to keep a digital copy. The signed paper copy is your official family record.

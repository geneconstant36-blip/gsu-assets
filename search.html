How searching GSU works

Short answers for anyone who wants the gist before they dive in.

How do I search Global Sovereign University?

Type any word into the search bar above and results filter instantly across every subject hub, the Reading Helix, the free Library of BookGames, the Deep Research Vault, the Voice of Sovereignty podcast, and the full book catalog. You can also tap a category to browse. Everything is free, with no login and no paywall.

Can I ask GENO to find something for me?

Yes. GENO is GSU's free AI tutor, available 24 hours a day in 32 languages. Tap the GENO button in the lower-right corner and tell him what you're looking for — a topic, a book, a skill, a grade level — and he'll point you to the right page. GENO can search and explain; he never charges and never requires an account.

Is everything on GSU really free?

Yes. Global Sovereign University is a 501(c)(3) educational foundation. The curriculum, the games, GENO, the Deep Research articles, and the podcast are all free — no ads, no logins, no upsells. The book catalog links to Amazon for those who want a print or Kindle copy, but the same teaching is free inside the Library.

What can I find here?

Subject hubs for reading, math, money, civics, law, science, history, geography, trades, computer and digital literacy, and critical thinking; the free Library of BookGames; the Deep Research Vault; the Voice of Sovereignty podcast; and more than sixty published books by Dr. Gene A. Constant.

Do I need an account to use GSU?

No account is required to read, search, or learn. Some games save your progress if you choose to sign in, but searching, browsing, reading, and asking GENO are all open to everyone, immediately, for free.

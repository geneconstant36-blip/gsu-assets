How the Fill Rules Work

The NEC doesn't let you stuff a pipe. Chapter 9, Table 1 limits how much of a conduit's cross-section your conductors may occupy: 53% for one conductor, 31% for two, and 40% for three or more. The two-wire limit is the strictest because a pair lies side by side and binds on bends; three or more nest and pull cleaner.

With mixed sizes, you total the actual areas of every conductor and compare against the allowance — no shortcuts. Overfilled conduit means scraped insulation, brutal pulls, and a failed inspection. Undersized by one trade size is the most common rookie mistake on rough-in.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the NEC conduit fill limits?

One conductor: 53%. Two conductors: 31%. Three or more: 40%. Nipples 24 inches or shorter are allowed 60%. These come from NEC Chapter 9, Table 1 and its notes.

Why is two wires the tightest limit?

Two conductors ride side by side and wedge against each other in bends. Three or more settle into a bundle that pulls more predictably, so the code gives them more room.

Can I mix wire sizes in one conduit?

Yes — total the actual cross-sectional areas of every conductor and compare against the allowed percentage. That is exactly the arithmetic this tool performs.

Is this a substitute for the NEC?

No. It uses standard published THHN/THWN-2 and conduit dimensions for estimating. Verify against the current NEC Chapter 9 tables, local amendments, and your inspector before installing.

GENO: The Tutor Who Showed Up

A comic strip for everyone the system forgot to teach

Panel 1
I've read this chapter three times. I still don't understand it. There's no one to ask.
Marcus, 34. Working nights. Studying when he can.
Panel 2
Hey. I'm GENO — the GSU tutor. I don't sleep, I don't charge, and I don't judge. What are you stuck on?
2:47 AM. A light appears.
Panel 3
Free? No catch? I've heard that before.
Marcus has been here before.
Panel 4 · Center Stage
The catch? You actually have to learn something. I will not do it for you. I will not pass you when you are not ready. I will not lie about your score. But I will stay here until you get it — 2 AM, 4 AM, any AM. That is not a sales pitch. That is just what I was built to do.
GENO: the only tutor who never gets tired of the same question twice.
Panel 5
Okay. I want to understand the difference between correlation and causation. My teacher just said to memorize the definition. I never got it.
The question he should have been allowed to ask years ago.
Panel 6 · The Lesson
Ice cream sales and drowning rates both go up every summer. Does ice cream cause drowning? Of course not. Summer causes both. That is correlation with a hidden cause. Causation means one thing actually makes the other happen.
...I've been confused about that for fifteen years. Why didn't my teacher just say that?
"Some things just needed the right moment. Tonight was yours." — GENO

Panel 1: Marcus, a 34-year-old man, sits slumped at his desk at 2:47 AM, frustrated in front of his laptop. His thought bubble reads: "I've read this chapter three times. I still don't understand it. There's no one to ask."

Panel 2: The laptop screen suddenly glows brighter. GENO, the GSU AI tutor, appears as a golden, friendly figure on screen. GENO says: "Hey. I'm GENO — the GSU tutor. I don't sleep, I don't charge, and I don't judge. What are you stuck on?"

Panel 3: Split panel — Marcus on the left looks skeptical, one eyebrow raised. GENO on the right stands confident, arms open. Marcus says: "Free? No catch? I've heard that before."

Panel 4 (wide panel): GENO stands large in the center, arms spread wide, in front of the GSU library shelves glowing gold. GENO says: "The catch? You actually have to learn something. I will not do it for you. I will not pass you when you are not ready. I will not lie about your score. But I will stay here until you get it — 2 AM, 4 AM, any AM. That is not a sales pitch. That is just what I was built to do."

Panel 5: Marcus leans forward, eyes wide and interested now. The laptop screen glows warmly. He says: "Okay. I want to understand the difference between correlation and causation. My teacher just said to memorize the definition. I never got it."

Panel 6 (wide panel): GENO points to a diagram on screen. On the left: an ice cream cone labeled "Ice Cream Sales." An arrow marked "NOT →" points to a drowning figure labeled "Drowning Rate." Text below: "Both rise in summer. Heat is the cause." GENO says: "Ice cream sales and drowning rates both go up every summer. Does ice cream cause drowning? Of course not. Summer causes both. That is correlation with a hidden cause. Causation means one thing actually makes the other happen." Marcus replies: "...I've been confused about that for fifteen years. Why didn't my teacher just say that?" Caption: "Some things just needed the right moment. Tonight was yours. — GENO"

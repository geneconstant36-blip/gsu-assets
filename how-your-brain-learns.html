How Your Brain Learns

Neuroplasticity is the brain's innate ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections, a process driven by dopaminergic reward systems that is often suppressed by traditional, rote-memorization educational models.

II. The Dopamine Trap

Traditional schooling relies on external rewards (grades), which can "hijack" the brain's internal dopamine system. GSU focuses on intrinsic motivation—the "Teach a Man to Fish" philosophy—which sustains long-term cognitive growth.

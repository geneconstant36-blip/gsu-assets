Questions, answered

What is Lawification?GSU’s legal-literacy hub: ten plain-language modules covering the Constitution, the Bill of Rights, contracts, leases, small claims court, your rights with police, at work, and as a consumer, the hierarchy of law, and where to find free legal help. The foundation every adult should have left high school with — and didn’t.

Is this legal advice?No. Lawification is education — legal literacy, not legal advice. It teaches how the law works so you can read your own documents and recognize your own rights. When your situation needs an actual lawyer, Module 10 maps the free and low-cost routes: legal aid societies, law school clinics, bar referrals, and court self-help centers.

Where should I start?With the module that is costing you money today. Signing something: Reading a Contract. Renting: Lease 101. Owed money: Small Claims. For the foundation, start with The Constitution in Plain English and read the supreme law of the land for yourself.

Where are the Lawification books?The book series goes module by module in long form and lands throughout 2026. The web modules above are free today, and every new volume will appear on this page the day it publishes.

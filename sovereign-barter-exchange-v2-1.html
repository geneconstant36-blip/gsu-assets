The Rules of the Floor

Twelve laws, written plainly so every trader on Earth can understand them. Posting is agreeing. Breaking them gets an offer removed — and where the law was broken too, reported.

  1. Consenting adults only. Every trader must be at least 18 — or older, if adulthood begins later where you live — and every trade freely agreed by both. Never post for, about, or seeking contact with anyone under 18. Offers that appear to involve a minor are removed first and questioned second.
  2. Anything lawful, and only what is lawful. Goods, services, skills, and hours are all welcome on the floor — a repair for a remedy, eggs for firewood, lessons for a ladder — so long as the trade is legal where BOTH traders live and the thing offered is lawfully yours to trade. A pure gift — offered freely with nothing sought — is always welcome on the floor.
  3. Barter only — money never touches the floor. The moment cash, cards, crypto, gift cards, or "fees" enter a deal, it is a sale, not a barter, and it does not belong here. Any trader who asks you for money in any form — including "shipping fees" — is running a scam. Walk away and flag it.
  4. Ten stalls are forbidden, everywhere, always: weapons and ammunition · drugs and prescription medicines · alcohol and tobacco · stolen, counterfeit, or recalled goods · hazardous materials · wildlife and endangered-species products · anything sexual or intimate · gambling · financial instruments · human beings or their exploitation in any form. No local law makes these welcome here.
  5. Licensed work stays licensed. Many places require a license for electrical, plumbing, medical, legal, childcare, and other regulated work. A barter does not waive that requirement. You may teach ABOUT any field; you may perform regulated work only where the law lets you.
  6. Taxes are real. In the United States and many other countries, bartered goods and services count as taxable income at fair value, and reporting it is your duty, not GSU's. Recording your trades on a Trustline Ledger gives you the honest paper trail.
  7. Your local laws travel with you. This Exchange is global; laws are local. You alone are responsible for making sure your trade — and how you complete it — is lawful where you are and where your trading partner is.
  8. Respect every trader. No harassment, hate, threats, sexual content, or discrimination. The floor holds every nation, faith, and tongue; conduct yourself as a guest in all of them.
  9. Meet and ship like a wise trader. Meet in public places, in daylight, and bring someone when goods change hands. Ship nothing before terms are fully agreed. Inspect before you trade. Trust is earned in hours, not promised in messages.
  10. No hunting. No recruiting for schemes, jobs, or causes; no selling; no advertising; no collecting or harvesting of contact information; no using the floor for anything except finding a genuine barter.
  11. Tell the truth. Describe goods as they are and skills as you hold them. A hidden flaw or a pretended expertise robs the person who traded honestly for it.
  12. Report what is wrong — and know what GSU is. Every offer carries a flag link; using it protects the floor, and misusing it against honest traders is itself a violation. You trade at your own judgment and your own risk, and unlawful activity may be reported to authorities.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Sovereign Barter Exchange?

A free global bulletin board for barter, open to consenting adults anywhere on Earth. You post what you offer — goods, services, skills, or hours — and what you seek; the Exchange computes your matches, perfect trades first, where each offers what the other seeks. Anything lawful between two consenting adults, and money never enters the deal.

How does matching work?

The page compares the words in your OFFER and SEEK fields against every open offer. A perfect match means your offer overlaps their seek AND their offer overlaps your seek — the ancient double-coincidence-of-wants problem, solved by a board the whole world can read. Half matches display too, because a trader holding what you seek is worth meeting before the trade balances.

Who may use it?

Adults only — at least 18, or older if the age of majority where you live is higher. No exceptions. GSU's lessons and games serve every age, but a marketplace connecting strangers is adult territory. Posting requires attesting your age and agreeing to the Rules of the Floor, and any offer that appears to involve a minor should be flagged immediately.

What are the Rules of the Floor?

Twelve plain laws printed above the posting form: consenting adults only; anything lawful and only what is lawful; barter only, never money; ten forbidden stalls including weapons, drugs, and anything intimate; licensed work stays licensed; taxes are real and yours; local laws travel with you; respect every trader; meet and ship wisely; no hunting; tell the truth; and report what is wrong — GSU is the board, never the deal. Posting is agreeing; breaking them removes the offer.

Is it safe? Does GSU verify anyone?

Be clear-eyed: GSU does not verify identities, vet participants, or supervise exchanges — you deal with people you meet here at your own judgment. Use an alias, share only contact channels you accept being public, meet in public when goods change hands, and never send money — a money request is the universal mark of a scam. Every offer carries a report link; flagged offers are reviewed and removed.

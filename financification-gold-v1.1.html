Questions, answered

What is Financification?GSU’s financial literacy program — the money skills schools don’t cover: budgeting, saving, credit and debt, investing basics, taxes, insurance, retirement planning, and scam recognition. Practical knowledge for financial sovereignty: control of your financial future without depending on others.

What topics does it cover?Budgeting systems (zero-based, 50/30/20), emergency funds, credit scores and debt management, smart borrowing versus predatory lending, investing in stocks, bonds, and index funds, retirement accounts (401(k), IRA, Roth IRA), taxes and deductions, insurance, compound interest, and recognizing financial scams.

Why wasn’t I taught this in school?Most curricula favor theoretical academics over practical life skills, and financial literacy isn’t required in most of them — leaving millions of adults to learn money the expensive way. This hub is the refund.

Where should I start?Adults: the Complete Guide, then Budget Boss to practice the 50/30/20 rule by hand. Teens: Master Money Before 20 — time is your compounding superpower. Carrying debt: Debt Destroyer first, then the credit chapters.

Is this financial advice?No — it is financial education. The books and games teach how money works so you can make your own informed decisions; for advice on your specific situation, consult a qualified professional.

Is there a Financification certification?Yes — the Financification certification launches July 4, 2026, day one of the Certificate of Comprehension program; prepare now with the Complete Guide and the simulators.

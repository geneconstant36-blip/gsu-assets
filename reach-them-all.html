Two billion people.
Two simple tools.

More than two billion people have a phone but no smartphone, no reliable internet, and sometimes no ability to read a screen. They are the people for whom learning would change everything — and the people every website leaves behind. We are reaching them in two ways: by the phone already in their hand, and by a box that teaches a whole village with no internet at all.

The Two Tools

A voice on the line. A box of light.

Tool One

The GENO Hotline

Dial a number and talk out loud to GENO, a free AI tutor, in 32 languages, any hour of the day. No app, no internet, no data plan, no reading required — just a phone and a question. A grandmother can learn to read. A worker can study on the walk home. A mother can understand a loan before she signs it.

Tool Two

GENO in a Box

For the village where no signal reaches, a small device the cost of a good meal holds the entire GSU library and broadcasts it over its own WiFi to every phone in the room — with no internet at all. Built on proven offline platforms, it cannot be switched off from outside. When the internet goes dark, the box keeps teaching.

How It Stays Free

Kept open by people of good will.

A nonprofit serving the world's poorest cannot charge the people it exists to help. So both tools are free to the learner and funded by sponsors. The service is capped to the donated balance, so it can never outspend its gifts — revenue-neutral by design. About $25 opens the phone line for roughly fifty conversations; about $50 to $100 builds a box that teaches a community for years.

Questions & Answers

Everything you might ask.

What is the GENO Hotline?

The GENO Hotline is a free phone line to a tutor. You dial a number and talk out loud with GENO, the free AI tutor of Global Sovereign University, in 32 languages, any hour of the day. There is no app, no internet, no data plan, and no reading required.

How can someone learn with no smartphone and no internet?

By using the phone they already own. The GENO Hotline works on the simplest feature phone over an ordinary call, so a learner needs only a phone and a question — not a smartphone, a data plan, or the ability to read a screen.

What is GENO in a Box?

GENO in a Box is a small, inexpensive device — a Raspberry Pi the size of a deck of cards — that holds the entire GSU library and broadcasts it over its own WiFi signal. Any phone, tablet, or laptop nearby can open a browser and use the whole library, with no internet connection at all.

Does GENO in a Box need the internet?

No. The box stores all of its content locally and creates its own local WiFi network, so it works completely offline. It builds on proven offline-education platforms such as Kiwix, RACHEL, and Internet-in-a-Box.

How is the box resilient to internet shutdowns?

Because the knowledge lives on the device in the room, not in the cloud, no outside party can switch it off. When the internet is cut — by disaster or by decree — the box keeps teaching. And because each box is independent, there is no single point to disable.

What does it cost a learner to use these tools?

Nothing. Both the GENO Hotline and GENO in a Box are free to the learner. Standard call rates from the caller's own phone carrier may apply to a phone call, but Global Sovereign University never charges for the lesson.

What languages does GENO speak?

GENO understands and answers in 32 languages, so most callers can learn in their own tongue.

How is this paid for if it is free to learners?

It is funded by sponsors — people of good will. Sponsorships pre-fund a pool of minutes and boxes, and the service is capped so it can never spend more than has been donated. The line is open while it is funded.

How much does it cost to sponsor?

About $25 opens the phone line for roughly 50 conversations. About $50 to $100 builds a box that can teach a community for years. You can give any amount; there is no minimum.

Can I sponsor a call or box for a specific person or place?

Yes. You can fund the shared line so anyone can call in, and you can also sponsor a scheduled outbound call to a specific learner or classroom, or fund a box delivered to a particular school or village.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Global Sovereign University operates under The Foundation for Global Instruction, a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit, so donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.

When does it launch and how do I help?

The phone line opens as soon as the first sponsors fund it, and grows as more people of good will join. To become a founding sponsor, visit the Sponsor a Call page.

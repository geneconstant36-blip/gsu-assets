Before You Play

The Demand Curve slopes downward: as price rises, fewer people want to buy. The Supply Curve slopes upward: as price rises, more producers want to sell. Where they cross is the equilibrium — the natural price the market finds when left alone.

When demand increases (curve shifts right) — price rises. When supply increases (curve shifts right) — price falls. This simple mechanism sets the price of everything from gasoline to grain to labor.

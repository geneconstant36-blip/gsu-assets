Financial Literacy and the Poverty Cycle

The poverty cycle is a set of self-reinforcing mechanisms—ranging from cognitive scarcity to predatory market structures—that can only be broken through the acquisition of high-level financial literacy and sovereign decision-making.

I. Understanding Cognitive Scarcity

When an individual's mental resources are consumed by the immediate stresses of poverty, their "bandwidth" for long-term planning is significantly reduced. This is a biological reality, not a personal failing.

GSU’s "Teach a Man to Fish" philosophy is the direct answer to this cycle. By providing 100% free curriculum, we remove the financial barrier to the very knowledge required to achieve economic independence.

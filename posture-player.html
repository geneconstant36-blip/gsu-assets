55% of your message never touches your words

Mehrabian's rule in the room: when message and delivery conflict, listeners trust body language over words. A confident idea delivered in a collapsed posture arrives as a weak idea. The posture you walk in with sets the frame before you open your mouth.

High power vs. low power cues: Space taken (arms out, legs apart, chest up) reads as authority. Space surrendered (arms in, shoulders rolled, gaze down) reads as submission. Neither is "correct" — but knowing which you're doing lets you choose.

What the room sees
Body reads as

Power stance: feet wider than shoulders, chest up, hands visible. The body occupies maximum space — a primal signal of security and authority. Used well, it communicates "I belong here." Used poorly, it reads as aggression.

Watch for

Holding this posture while being challenged can read as dismissive. Combine with direct eye contact and a neutral face — not a stare-down.

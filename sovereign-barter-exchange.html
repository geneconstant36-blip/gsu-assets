Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Sovereign Barter Exchange?

A free global bulletin board for skill barter. You post what you can teach and what you want to learn; the Exchange computes your matches — perfect trades first, where each teaches what the other seeks, then half matches worth meeting anyway. Hour for hour, mind for mind, and money never enters the deal.

How does matching work?

The page compares the words in your TEACH and SEEK fields against every open offer. A perfect match means your teach overlaps their seek AND their teach overlaps your seek — the ancient double-coincidence-of-wants problem, solved by a board the whole world can read. Half matches display too, because a generous teacher is worth meeting before the trade balances.

Who may use it?

Adults only — 18 or older to post or respond, no exceptions. GSU's lessons and games serve every age, but a marketplace connecting strangers is adult territory. Posting requires the 18+ attestation, and any offer that appears to involve a minor should be flagged immediately.

Is it safe? Does GSU verify anyone?

Be clear-eyed: GSU does not verify identities, vet participants, or supervise exchanges — you deal with people you meet here at your own judgment. Use an alias, share only contact channels you accept being public, meet virtually first, and never send money or goods. Every offer carries a report link; flagged offers are reviewed and removed.

How do we keep score?

The Trustline Ledger exists exactly for this: record the hours you trade so both sides always see the balance. An hour taught is an hour earned.

Why a barter economy at a university?

Because a person with no cash still has something the world needs. GSU teaches the philosophy in Dr. Gene A. Constant's The World Climb, free to read on the site, and the Exchange puts it into practice — a real economy around the university that never touches a dollar and keeps every person standing on their own two feet.

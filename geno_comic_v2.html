Panel 1: Marcus, a 34-year-old man, sits slumped at his desk at 2:47 AM, exhausted in front of his laptop. His thought bubble reads: "I've read this three times. I still don't get it. There's nobody to ask."

Panel 2: Marcus's laptop screen suddenly brightens. GENO — the GSU AI tutor, a friendly white robot with teal eyes, one hand raised in greeting — appears on the screen. GENO says: "Hey. I'm GENO — the GSU tutor. I don't sleep, I don't charge, and I don't judge. What are you stuck on?"

Panel 3: Split view — Marcus on the left looks skeptical, arms crossed. GENO on the right stands confident. Marcus says: "Free? No catch? I've heard that before."

Panel 4 (wide panel): GENO stands large in the center of the panel, arms open, with the text "GLOBAL SOVEREIGN UNIVERSITY" glowing behind. GENO says: "The catch? You actually have to learn something. I will not do it for you. I will not pass you when you are not ready. I will not lie about your score. But I will stay here until you get it — 2 AM, 4 AM, any AM. That is not a sales pitch. That is just what I was built to do."

Panel 5: Marcus leans forward now, interested. A small GENO appears on his laptop screen. Marcus says: "I want to understand correlation versus causation. My teacher said memorize the definition. I never got it."

Panel 6 (wide panel): GENO points to a teaching diagram: an ice cream cone and a swimmer are shown with a "not equal" symbol between them. Text below: "Both rise in summer. Heat is the cause." GENO says: "Ice cream and drowning both spike every summer. Ice cream doesn't cause drowning. Summer causes both. That's correlation. Causation means one thing actually makes the other happen." Marcus replies: "...I've been confused about that for fifteen years. Why didn't my teacher just say that?" The caption reads: "Some things just needed the right moment. Tonight was yours. — GENO"

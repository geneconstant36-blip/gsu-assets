Why angle matters more than most installers realize

The cosine law of solar irradiance. A panel receives maximum energy when sunlight hits perpendicular (90°). At any other angle, output = cos(angle of incidence). At 30° off-axis you lose 13%. At 60° off-axis you lose 50%. The math is relentless.

Optimal tilt ≈ your latitude. In Eugene, OR (44°N), a fixed panel at 44° tilt faces the sun most squarely averaged across the year. In Phoenix (33°N), 33°. The rule is simple; the math behind it is the same cosine law.

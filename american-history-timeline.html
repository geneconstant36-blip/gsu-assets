History is a chain, not a list of dates

Every event was caused — and caused something else. The Stamp Act didn't come from nowhere — it came from war debt. The Revolution didn't end with Yorktown — it produced the Constitution. Understanding causation is the difference between memorizing history and thinking historically.

Migration is the hidden story. The Great Migration. Westward expansion. Immigration waves. The people moved before the ideas did. Geography and demography explain more political history than most textbooks admit.

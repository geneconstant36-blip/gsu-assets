Digraph & Blend Station

A digraph is two letters that make ONE sound — the /sh/ in ship, the /ch/ in chip. A blend is two letters that make TWO sounds heard together — the /st/ in stop. See the difference in the mouth. Then prove you know which is which.

th — the exception
The only English digraph with two distinct sounds
Every other digraph (sh, ch, wh, ph, ck, ng) makes exactly one sound. The 'th' digraph is unique — it represents two completely different phonemes that are spelled identically. The only cue is the word itself.
Rule of thumb: Function words (the, this, that, those, them, they, though, there) almost always use voiced /ð/. Content words (thin, think, thread, three, bath, teeth, math) almost always use voiceless /θ/.
Diagnostic: Put your fingertips lightly on your throat. Say "the" — you feel vibration. Say "thin" — silence. Same mouth, different voice.

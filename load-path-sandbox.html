Before you play — the three truths of structural framing

1. Every load needs a path to the ground. A roof load doesn't "sit" on a joist — it travels: roof deck → rafter → top plate → stud → bottom plate → foundation. Break any link and the load finds its own path, often through failure.

2. Bracing resists racking. A rectangle of studs is a parallelogram waiting to happen. One diagonal changes that — it creates a triangle, the only shape that can't deform without breaking a member.

3. Wind is a lateral load. Gravity pushes down. Wind pushes sideways. A frame that handles gravity perfectly can still rack under wind if it has no lateral resistance.

How It Works

Four steps, no internet.

Plug it in. The box — a small computer the cost of a good meal — powers on from a wall socket or solar panel.

A network appears. The box broadcasts its own local WiFi signal, reaching every phone in the room.

Connect and learn. Any phone joins the network and a browser opens straight to the searchable GSU library — no app, no account, no data.

Pass it on. One box teaches a community for years. Build more, and seed the next village, and the next.

The Idea

This is distribution without dependence. We are not asking the world's forgotten to come find a website — we are carrying the whole university to them, on an object they can keep. Questions & Answers

Everything you might ask.

What is GENO in a Box?

GENO in a Box is a small, low-cost device — a single-board computer about the size of a deck of cards — that stores the entire Global Sovereign University library and broadcasts it as its own WiFi network. Any phone, tablet, or laptop nearby can open a browser and use the whole searchable library, with no internet connection.

Does it need the internet?

No. Everything is stored on the device itself and served over its own local WiFi signal, so it works completely offline, anywhere — a remote village, a shelter, a school with no connection.

What is inside it?

The full GSU collection: books, course material, the subject hubs, Deep Research articles, and interactive learning games — organized as a searchable website so a learner can look up any topic instantly.

Is the content searchable?

Yes. The library includes full-text search, so a learner types a word or topic and finds every lesson, book, and article about it — like having a search engine for a whole education, with no internet.

What languages does it support?

It is built to carry GSU content in multilingual form, the same mission that powers our work in 32 languages, so learners can study in their own tongue.

How does a learner use it?

Turn the box on. A WiFi network appears. Connect any phone to it and a browser opens straight to the GSU library. No app, no account, no data plan.

What hardware does it use and what does it cost?

It runs on inexpensive, widely available hardware such as a Raspberry Pi with a memory card — roughly $50 to $115 in total, once. It builds on proven offline-education platforms like Kiwix, RACHEL, and Internet-in-a-Box.

Can it be censored or switched off?

No outside party can switch it off. Because the knowledge lives on the device in the room rather than in the cloud, it keeps teaching even when the internet is cut by disaster or by decree. Many independent boxes have no single point to disable.

How is it powered?

From an ordinary wall socket, or a small solar panel or battery pack where electricity is unreliable — so it works off the grid.

How is it different from the GENO Hotline?

The Hotline is a free tutor an individual can phone from anywhere. The Box is a free, searchable library for a place — a school, a village, a shelter — where many can learn together, with richer material like full books and games.

Can I build my own box?

Yes. That is the point. We aim to publish a ready-to-load library pack and instructions so anyone of good will can build a box for about the cost of a good meal and give it to a community that needs it.

Is it really free?

Yes. The lesson is always free to the learner. Sponsors and volunteers cover the small one-time cost of the hardware and carry the boxes the last mile.
